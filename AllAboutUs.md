### The Adaptive Algorithm Explained

**Input Signals:**
- Accuracy on questions

- Number of attempts

- Learning velocity (how fast you improve)

**Output Adjustments:**
- Content difficulty
- Number of practice problems
- Type of content (video vs. text vs. interactive)
- Pacing recommendations

### Real Example

**Scenario:** Sarah learning quadratic equations

**Week 1:** Sarah answers 10 questions. Gets 4 correct, 6 wrong.
- System analysis: Struggling with factorization method
- Adjustment: More videos on factorization, 20 extra practice problems, slower pace

- System analysis: Factorization improving, but still weak on completing the square
- Adjustment: Targeted practice on completing the square, reduce factorization practice

**Mastery (85%):**

## SECTION 6: The Science Behind the Results

### Section Title
"Why Mastery-Based Learning Works"

### Evidence Base

**Our approach is built on decades of learning science research:**

**1. Mastery Learning (Bloom, 1968)**
- Research shows 90% of students can master any subject given adequate time and appropriate instruction
- Traditional fixed-pace instruction only allows ~30% to reach mastery
- EduMeUp's adaptive pacing implements Bloom's mastery principles

**2. Spaced Repetition (Ebbinghaus, 1885; Cepeda et al., 2006)**
- Distributed practice dramatically improves retention
- Our algorithm spaces reviews at optimal intervals
- Prevents the "cram and forget" cycle

**3. Formative Assessment (Black & Wiliam, 1998)**
- Continuous feedback improves outcomes by 0.4-0.7 standard deviations
- Our diagnostic and mastery checks provide constant feedback
- Students know exactly where they stand at all times

**4. Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky, 1978)**
- Learning is maximized when difficulty is "just right"
- Our adaptive algorithm keeps students in their optimal learning zone
- Not too easy (boredom), not too hard (frustration)

**5. Self-Regulated Learning (Zimmerman, 2002)**
- Students who monitor their own progress learn better
- Our dashboards promote metacognitive awareness
- Students become active managers of their learning
